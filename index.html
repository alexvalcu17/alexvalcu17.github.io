Problem

Farm management is labor-intensive, requiring substantial human involvement for scheduling breeding and vaccinations — tasks that could be automated. Due to the lack of dedicated management tools, farms rarely reach their full production potential.

Solution

We provide an integrated breeding and vaccination management system that notifies farmers and creates customized schedules based on their specific farm data. Additionally, our platform offers performance metrics visualization, empowering farmers with insights to make decisions for optimizing production.

Value proposition

We give farmers the freedom to focus on their livestock by significantly reducing the time they previously spent managing breeding and vaccination schedules.

How we identified the problem?

During initial research, both on the internet and informally with some neighbours living at the countryside who own a couple of animals, we found out that the farm management process is still done manually, with a lot of paperwork and human involvement. While some farmers were meticulous, many relied on ad-hoc methods to remember vaccination schedules, track breeding cycle, maintain animal health records, and monitor production metrics. For these, even managing a small number of animals became a challenge, with inbreeding prevention, accurate record-keeping and care scheduling emerging as frequent issues. The core problem centered around the need for a centralized, automated solution that could simplify record keeping and routine management tasks, making it easier for farmers to manage their farms.

What we thought is a solution?

Our initial solution idea was an app designed to streamline and automate the tracking of breeding, vaccinations, and health data for animal breeders - cows, horses, sheep, pigs, rabbits and others. Key proposed features included relative information about the animal, automatic reminders for vaccination dates, lineage tracking to prevent inbreeding, centralized storage of animal profiles, and a community-focused marketplace for buying and selling animals. We anticipated that the app could reduce the need for manual memory recall or paper records, creating a more organized and accessible management system.

Customer discovery plan

To verify our understanding of the farmers' challenges and test our solution's potential, we planned a structured customer discovery process:


Segment Identification


We focused on small-scale farmers who may not use advanced tracking methods but still face organizational challenges.

Outreach


We reached potential customers through community groups, social media, and word-of-mouth, targeting farmers interested in simplifying their processes.

Methodology


Our data collection included semi-structured interviews, using a set of open-ended questions to understand both their current practices and pain points, and to gauge their interest in our proposed features.

Objectives


We aimed to confirm the need for automation in basic rabbit care management and understand what specific features would be most helpful.

Future development


To attract people interested in our idea, we decided to have a sign-up option for email updates on our landing page. These will be used to keep customers informed about what we offer. Social media is also a path to follow in order to increase our visibility and popularity.

Process in detail

Having all these set, we started looking for people who would be interested in our idea. We used social media platforms such as Facebook, Whatsapp and Messenger to connect with potential customers from Farming Groups or connections of us or our friends who own animals. We managed to have a couple of discussions with horse, sheep, cow and rabbit owners, both virtually and face-to-face, adding a few of the evidence we gathered to our Google Drive. We even managed to visit a rabbit farm! With this visit, we found out that the owner will be very interesed in being our Unfair Advantage, as he is holding an Youtube channel with more than 5.8k subscribers and he is willing to promote our app on his channel, as well as give us contacts about other rabbit breeders he knows.

We tried to adopt an approach which is indicated in the Mom Test book, and so we decided on 3 main questions to be asked during the interviews and some additional questions depending on the answers we received. We also tried to keep the conversation as natural as possible, to make the interviewee feel comfortable and to get the most honest answers.

The main questions were:



How do you currently track your information such as breeding, vaccination schedule and many others for animals?

Which management activities take up most of your time?

Have you ever encountered issues in keeping track of when and which vaccines the animals have received?


The potential customers who agreed to give us interviews provided valuable insights into their needs and preferences. We discovered that, while some breeders were highly organized and maintained detailed records, many were seeking a simpler and more automated approach to managing basic tasks like breeding, vaccinations, and feed. Several interviewees indicated that they currently use ad-hoc methods to track breeding cycles and vaccinations, relying on handwritten notes or basic files on their computer. They highlighted the potential value of features such as automated reminders for vaccinations and breeding events, which would reduce their dependency on memory and prevent missed care windows.


Moreover, a recurring theme was the challenge of keeping track of family lineage to avoid inbreeding, particularly for those who don't have a structured record-keeping system. An in-app family lineage tracker with alerts for related pairings was widely seen as beneficial, making it easier to sustain healthy breeding practices without additional manual work. We also noted a strong interest in the ability to centralize information on each animal’s lineage, breed, and other relevant data in a digital profile.


Beyond animal care, the interviewees expressed interest in an integrated animal-specific marketplace (in our case, the accent was on rabbits as we had more insights on them). Unlike general platforms where listings can be lost among unrelated products, a dedicated marketplace with verified animal profiles would simplify buying and selling, improving transparency and connecting breeders more directly.


Also, there were some interviews that were not as insightful as we hoped. However, we learnt from them that our project could also help the small-scale breeders. The ones who participated at our interviews, were not reluctant to using a simple app, even if they are not as invested as the others into the rabbit livestock. Therefore, the idea of releasing the app with different subscription plans appeared.

How these insights affected our product?

Rabbits first


Considering the insights and our Unfair Advantage, we decided on making a top priority adding all the necessary functionalities for rabbits.

Subscription Model


Recognizing the different levels of investment and engagement among breeders, we decided to offer a tiered subscription model.

Tracking and Reminders


Given the popularity of automated reminders, we prioritized developing a user-friendly notification system for vaccinations, breeding cycles, and general care tasks.

Lineage and Inbreeding


We decided to integrate a lineage tracker with inbreeding alerts as a core feature, given the frequent challenges breeders faced in managing family connections within their herds.

Marketplace Integration


We decided to add a roadmap feature for a dedicated rabbit(at first) marketplace within the app, with filters for lineage, health data, and verified profiles, catering to both breeders and buyers.

Ready for customer validation stage?

We believe that we are prepared to enter the Customer Validation stage to test actual interest in purchasing the app. We have a solid understanding of the main problems and have tailored our product to address these needs with features like centralized profiles, automated reminders, and a dedicated marketplace. Feedback from initial interviews suggests that there is a demand for these tools, particularly the basic tracking and reminder features. The positive response toward the app's simplicity and automation indicates strong potential for customer adoption.


Our next steps will include testing the app with a beta group of interested breeders to gauge usability, willingness to pay, and refine the subscription model based on real-world usage. This phase will allow us to validate our solution's effectiveness and further adapt based on user feedback.
